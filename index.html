Key Wallet Share Findings
💰 $1.65B Deposit Opportunity — Highest-ROI Action⑤
268 customers where the bank already holds >50% of lending wallet share but <20% of deposit share. These companies trust the bank with their debt but route their treasury elsewhere. Total cash on balance sheet for this group = $5.5B. A TD or sweep deposit proposition requires no new credit risk and no new customer acquisition.
Lending WS Materially Stronger Than Deposit WS⑥
The raw Wallet Share columns contain extreme outliers (>100%) due to near-zero denominators. Median values (capped 0–100%) show Deposit WS ≈ 2% and Lending WS ≈ 0% — reflecting that most customers have partial data only. The qualitative gap between lending and deposit capture is the actionable insight, not the average figures.
3,902 Growth Targets — But Insufficient Data to Act
The largest quadrant — low lending and low deposit share — also has the most missing balance sheet data. Coverage teams must complete financial profiling on these accounts before this can become a growth priority.

The Real Risk Is Concentration, Not Credit
Net loss of $4.19M against a $1.74B GP pool is still small (0.24%). Credit quality is not the primary threat. Revenue concentration remains the dominant risk — 77.8% of GP from the top 10% of customers means one large client departure matters far more than any individual loan default.
⚠ Sub-Cost-of-Capital Lending — 54.8% of Clean Loan Book
1,037 of 1,893 qualifying loan customers sit below 5% ROE — almost certainly below SMBC's cost of equity. Median ROE of 4.2% confirms that more than half the loan book is sub-threshold. Repricing review is a capital efficiency imperative.

Defend Branch S — Now
$1,066M from one branch is a single point of failure. If the top 50 Branch S clients reduced activity by 20%, the bank would lose more GP than all of branches N, Y, and V combined. Lock in long-term deposit agreements, increase product depth, and make switching costly before a competitor makes it easy.
Scale Branch B — Best Unit Economics
Branch B has the deepest relationship profile (2.6 avg products) and highest mean ROE at 16.0% — the model for what good looks like. Growing B customer count by 20% would add ~$52M in GP and meaningfully reduce S dependency.
Branch N — The Dormancy Opportunity
88.3% dormancy on 6,413 total customers vs 40.6% for Branch S. Only 748 are active. If Branch N's active rate were raised to the portfolio average (47.2%), approximately 2,276 additional customers would be activated. At Branch N's average GP of $77K, that represents a ~$175M upside requiring zero new acquisition.
